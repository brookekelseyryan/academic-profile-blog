Interests

I’m ever-fascinated with the intersection of problems that arise from the ubiquity of technology, and the unique problems that are inevitably generated in the crosshairs. As a teacher, this fascination manifests by eagerly communicating the beauty inherent in science and computing and instilling a similar joy of learning in my students. As a scientist, I am mesmerized by the process of developing social-computational models which capture the fluidity and indefinable qualities of our humanity. I desire a multifaceted career as both a college professor and researcher, which combines my fascination for Human-Computer Interaction with highly technical subdomains of computing.

The complex, technical problems of our society do not heed the boundaries set within the confines of a singular academic discipline, and the solutions to such problems demand equally nuanced and interdisciplinary approaches. I’ve established a set of guiding questions from which I seek to build the foundation of the next phase of my career goals to become both a college professor and researcher:

What problems in Computer Science necessitate deep computational, technical knowledge but lack human-computer interaction focused research? Furthermore, which problems can be revolutionized with a practical, industry-applicable, human-centered perspective? Do current educational methods or organizational structures in computing prohibit innovative perspectives? And crucially, how can we develop scalable, data-driven solutions which facilitate learning and promote diversity and inclusion in highly specialized fields of computing?
